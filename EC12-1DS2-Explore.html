A. Key Terminology Flashcards

Click a card to flip it and reveal the definition.

Data Swamp

A data lake that is poorly managed, lacks metadata, and is difficult to navigate, making the data within it effectively useless.

Metadata

"Data about data". It describes the content, quality, condition, and other characteristics of data (e.g., date created, author, file size).

Sampling Bias

Occurs when the data collected does not accurately represent the entire population, leading to skewed or incorrect analysis results.

ICIP

Indigenous Cultural and Intellectual Property. Rights to heritage, traditional knowledge, and cultural expression (e.g., stories, designs, plants).

OAIC

Office of the Australian Information Commissioner. The independent national regulator for privacy and freedom of information.

Data Signal

Cues (like notifications or 'like' counts) designed to trigger a behavioural response from a user, often utilizing psychological biases.

Question 1 (Legislation)

Describe the main purpose of the Privacy Act 1988 and its role in protecting individuals' data in Australia.

Model Answer: The Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) regulates how personal information is handled by Australian Government agencies and large private sector organisations. Its main purpose is to balance the free flow of information with an individual's right to privacy. It achieves this through the 13 Australian Privacy Principles (APPs), which set out standards for the collection, use, disclosure, and security of personal data.

Question 2 (Influence of Data)

Explain how curated and communicated data can subtly influence a user’s decision making? Use an example.

Model Answer: Curated data involves filtering and presenting specific information to tell a narrative, often omitting context. This influences decisions by creating "Filter Bubbles" or using social signals.

Example: A travel website showing "3 people are looking at this hotel right now" (a data signal) creates a sense of urgency (scarcity bias), influencing the user to book immediately without comparing prices elsewhere.

Question 3 (ICIP)

How can organisations uphold Indigenous Cultural and Intellectual Property (ICIP) principles when handling cultural data?

Model Answer: Organisations can uphold ICIP by following the principle of Indigenous Data Sovereignty. This involves:
1. Consultation: Seeking permission from Traditional Owners before collecting data.
2. Control: Allowing Indigenous communities to control how their data is stored and used.
3. Benefit: Ensuring the data use provides a direct benefit to the community.
4. Acknowledgement: Correctly attributing the source of the cultural knowledge.
