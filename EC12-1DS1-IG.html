1. Data Fundamentals

Before analysing data, we must understand its basic forms and characteristics. This section covers the essential classifications of data, from its type and structure to its levels of measurement, providing the foundation for all data-related tasks.

Quantitative vs. Qualitative

Quantitative (Numerical)

Measures objective quantities. Answers "how many?" or "how much?". Represented by numbers (integers, floats).

Qualitative (Categorical)

Describes qualities or characteristics. Answers "what kind?" or "why?". Represented by strings, booleans, or categories.

Structured vs. Unstructured

Structured Data

Highly organised in a fixed format, like rows and columns in a spreadsheet or database.

Unstructured Data

No predefined model, such as text in emails, social media posts, images, and videos.

2. Data Collection & Quality

The value of any analysis depends entirely on the quality of the data collected. Here, we explore how data is gathered and the critical criteria‚ÄîRelevance, Accuracy, Validity, and Reliability (RAVR)‚Äîused to assess its trustworthiness and guard against bias.

Data Sampling Methods

Active Collection: User knowingly provides data (e.g., filling out a survey).

Passive Collection: Data gathered in the background (e.g., website cookies tracking clicks).

Assessing Data Quality (RAVR)

  • Relevance: Does it apply to your problem?
  • Accuracy: Is the data correct and error-free?
  • Validity: Does it measure what it claims to measure?
  • Reliability: Is the collection method consistent?

The Impact of Data Bias

Bias can systematically skew data, leading to unfair or inaccurate conclusions. Understanding its forms is key to mitigating it.

5. Privacy & Security

In our digital world, protecting personal data is crucial. Convenience features in software can often introduce privacy risks. Understanding these features helps you make informed decisions to safeguard your information.

Software Features Affecting Privacy

Autofill

Saves personal info in browsers, which can be exposed if a device is compromised.

Public Connections

Data sent over public Wi-Fi can be intercepted (man-in-the-middle attacks).

Checkboxes

Often pre-ticked to gain consent for data sharing or marketing by default.

Terms of Agreement

Lengthy documents where users unknowingly agree to excessive data collection.
