The Enterprise Project Lifecycle

A visual toolkit for Year 12 Enterprise Computing students to understand the four key stages of developing a large-scale system, from initial idea to final evaluation.

Stage 1

Identifying and Defining

This foundational stage is about understanding the problem, defining project scope, and setting up the project for success. It's where questions are asked and the core purpose is established.

The Iterative Cycle

Instead of a single straight line, projects often run in cycles of refinement. This allows for flexibility and continuous improvement.

Stage 2

Researching and Planning

This stage is about deep thinking and structured planning. It involves exploring solutions, creating detailed plans, and using visual tools to map out the project.

Design & Systems Thinking

Using different mental models: computational (breaking down problems), design (user needs), and systems (holistic view).

Stage 4

Testing and Evaluating

The final stage ensures the system works correctly and meets user needs. It involves rigorous testing, gathering feedback, and planning for the system's future.

Verification vs. Validation

Verification

🔎

"Are we building the product right?"

Does it meet all the technical specifications and requirements?

Validation

💬

"Are we building the right product?"

Does it actually solve the user's problem and meet their needs?
