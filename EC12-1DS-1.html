The Data Science Lifecycle

From raw numbers to actionable insights, data goes on a journey. This process ensures that conclusions are reliable and meaningful.

1. Collection

Gathering raw data from primary (surveys, sensors) or secondary (reports, databases) sources using active or passive methods.

2. Storage & Processing

Storing data in databases or data warehouses, then cleaning and preparing it by filtering, sorting, and handling errors.

3. Analysis

Using techniques like data mining, statistical modelling, or machine learning to uncover patterns, correlations, and insights.

4. Presentation

Communicating findings through visualisations like charts, dashboards, and infographics to tell a clear and compelling story.

Quality is Everything

Poor quality data leads to flawed conclusions. It's crucial to be aware of ethical issues and potential for bias. Under the Privacy Act 1988, organisations have a legal duty to protect personal information.

Bias can unintentionally skew results, making them unfair or inaccurate. Understanding its sources is the first step to mitigating it.

Making Sense of It All: Analysis Techniques

Different tools are used to analyse data depending on the goal. Statistical models seek to explain, while machine learning aims to predict.

Technique Primary Goal Example Use
Statistical Modelling Explain relationships between variables ("Why?"). Analysing survey data to see if age impacts purchasing habits.
Machine Learning Make predictions based on patterns in data ("What will happen next?"). Forecasting future weather patterns based on historical data.

Telling the Story with Data Visualisation

Choosing the right chart is key to clear communication. The NESA syllabus requires you to be proficient with several types, each with its own strength.

Bar Chart: Comparing Categories

Ideal for comparing values across different groups.

Line Chart: Showing Trends

Perfect for tracking changes over a period of time.

Pie Chart: Showing Proportions

Best for showing parts of a whole (use with few categories).
